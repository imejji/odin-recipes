Banana Bread

Description

Hi y'all. It's been very cold and windy here for me, and I start to crave foods with warm spices during this time of year. One of my favorite things to bake is banana bread. The aroma and warmth of the cinnamon and cloves combined with the buttery richness and sweetness of brown sugar and banana bring me a lot of comfort, and I hope it does the same for you.

Ingredients

  • 205 g bread flour
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/8 tsp ground cloves
  • 113 g softened butter
  • 150 g brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs + 1 yolk (at room temp)
  • 5 large very ripened bananas
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • Optional: 100 g roasted and chopped walnuts

Steps

Prep:

  1. Preheat oven to 350F.
  2. Prepare three bowls, one bread pan, a hand mixer, and optionally one baking sheet for roasting walnuts.
  3. If roasting walnuts: chop 100 g of walnuts and place onto baking sheet, spacing them out evenly. After oven is heated, roast for 7-10 minutes. Keep an eye on them to ensure they don't burn. Once roasted, take walnuts out and set aside to begin preparing the batter. Do not turn off the oven yet.

Mixing:

  1. In one bowl, whisk together dry ingredients and set aside.
  2. In another bowl, mash bananas with a fork until smooth and set aside.
  3. In final bowl, add softened butter and brown sugar. Using a hand mixer, cream them together until light and smooth.
  4. On medium speed, add the eggs and yolk one at a time to the creamed butter and sugar until mixed well.
  5. On low speed, mix in mashed bananas and vanilla extract to the rest of the wet mixture.
  6. Now slowly mix in the dry ingredients until no pockets of flour remain. Take care not to overmix!
  7. Finally, if you roasted walnuts, gently fold them into the mixture.

Baking:

  1. Oil or butter the bread pan and spoon in the final mixture.
  2. Bake on the center rack for about 50min - 1hr, checking in to make sure it doesn't burn.
  3. At the 50min mark, check with a fork or toothpick. There should be little to no dough left over when it's ready.
  4. Remove from oven and allow to cool for a while so it can settle properly.
  5. Cut and serve.

Preserving

Cover and store at room temp for up to 2 days. Banana bread can also be refrigerated up to 1 week or tightly wrapped and frozen up to 4 months. Thaw frozen loaves, wrapped or unwrapped, in the fridge or at room temp.

Enjoy! - Jay
