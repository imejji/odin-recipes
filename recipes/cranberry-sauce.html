Cranberry Sauce

Description

Hi y'all. Sometimes I don't wanna wait for November to get into the fall dishes. One of my favorite dishes to make outside the fall season is a spiced cranberry sauce. This recipe is based on an Aunt's recipe, but with some modifications, including using mace instead of the more potent nutmeg. If you don't have access to mace you can subsitute with nutmeg to your tastes. I've sometimes had this warm, but it does kinda taste better chilled or room tempurature in some situations, such as being used in a leftovers sandwich with dressing.

Ingredients

  • One 340g bag of fresh or frozen cranberries
  • 1 zested orange with its squeezed orange juice
  • 150g water
  • 150g brown sugar
  • One stick of cinnamon
  • pinch of ground cloves, ground mace, and salt
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

Prep:

  1. Rinse cranberries in cold water. Do not thaw them if frozen.
  2. Pour the water, brown sugar, and 1/2 of the rinsed cranberries into a medium saucepan
  3. Zest the orange and set aside orange zest and vanilla for later.
  4. Cut the zested orange in half and squeeze the juice into the saucepan

Cooking:

  1. Heat mixture on medium heat while stirring occasionally until it comes to a simmer.
  2. Reduce the heat to lowish and keep occasionally stirring for about 10 minutes.
  3. Soon, you will see cranberries burst and the sauce thicken as the liquid reduces.
  4. Once thickened to your liking (Don't overcook!), turn off heat and remove saucepan.
  5. Let cool and serve or chill before serving if you like.

Preserving

Cranberry sauce can be refridgerated for up to five days.

Enjoy! - Jay
